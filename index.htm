Gauging the Global Conversation

Public Sentiment on the Open Quantum Institute

Overall Sentiment

Since its launch, the Open Quantum Institute (OQI) has generated a predominantly positive and hopeful online conversation, focusing on its mission to harness quantum computing for global challenges.

65% Positive
30% Neutral
5% Negative

Where is the Conversation Happening?

Discourse varies significantly across different online platforms, reflecting the diverse audiences engaging with the OQI's mission, from technical experts to the general public.

What Are People Talking About?

The online discussion revolves around several key themes, with topics like global collaboration and societal impact driving the most engagement and positive sentiment.

Key Discussion Themes

A thematic analysis reveals a strong focus on the OQI's potential to solve real-world problems through its unique collaborative model. The connection to the UN's Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) is a particularly strong driver of positive engagement.

  • Societal Impact (SDGs): Overwhelmingly positive focus on using quantum for good.
  • Collaboration & Open Science: High praise for the inclusive, multi-stakeholder approach.
  • Pace of Innovation: Hopeful but measured discussion, acknowledging the long-term nature of quantum R&D.
  • Global Accessibility: Positive sentiment around democratizing access to quantum resources.

Sentiment Trend Over Time

Public interest has peaked around key milestones, such as the official launch and major announcements. The overall sentiment has remained consistently positive throughout the initial pilot phase.

Data Sources

The analysis presented in this infographic is a synthesis of information and sentiment gathered from a wide array of public online sources between March 2024 and October 2025. Key sources include:

  • Official Communications: Press releases, articles, and white papers published on the Open Quantum Institute and CERN official websites.
  • Tech & Science Journalism: In-depth articles and reports from specialized publications such as The Quantum Insider and other leading technology news outlets.
  • Academic & Research Communities: Discussions, pre-prints, and white papers from platforms like arXiv, and publications from collaborating institutions.
  • Professional Networks & Social Media: Analysis of discourse on platforms including LinkedIn, X (formerly Twitter), and specialized scientific forums to gauge professional and public sentiment.
  • Partner & Stakeholder Publications: Announcements and blog posts from key partners like GESDA, UBS, and participating UN organizations.
